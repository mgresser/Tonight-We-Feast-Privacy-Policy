Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 6, 2026

Welcome to Tonight We Feast. Your privacy and data sovereignty are fundamental to our mission of providing a premium, seamless meal planning and cooking experience. This Privacy Policy details how we handle your information across our web application, browser extension, and mobile applications.

1. Information We Collect

We believe in minimal data collection. We only process information that is directly necessary to deliver the features of the application:

  • Account Authentication: If you choose to sync your recipe box across devices, we use Google Sign-In via Firebase Auth. This provides us with your basic public profile information (UID, email address, profile photo, and name).
  • Recipe Data: Custom recipes you write, dictations you type, scraped recipes, and associated cooked images are stored in your private client-side browser storage (LocalStorage) and synchronized to your private Cloud Firestore collection under users/{uid}/recipes.
  • Planner & Household Data: Serving counts, leftover projections, scheduled dishes, and diner tracking checklist choices are stored and synced to keep your menu plan up-to-date.

2. Adherence to Data Privacy Standards

All development and features in the Tonight We Feast ecosystem adhere to strict data privacy principles:

  • Data Ownership: You own all your data. We do not sell, license, or monetize your recipes, pictures, or eating habits.
  • No Third-Party Sharing: We do not share your account or meal schedule data with any advertising networks, trackers, or external entities.
  • Local-First Fallback: If you choose not to sign in, all data remains entirely local to your device’s LocalStorage and is never transmitted to our servers.

3. Security & Hosting

Synchronization is powered securely via Google Firebase and Hosted on Google Cloud Platform (GCP) Cloud Run. All communications between the client applications, Chrome Extension, and Firebase/GCP databases are encrypted via Secure Socket Layer (SSL/TLS).

4. Data Deletion and De-authorization

You can clear your local database at any time by clearing your browser cache/storage or uninstalling the applications. If you wish to delete your synchronized account and Firestore data, you can do so by contacting support or clearing your synced recipe box within the application settings.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page.
